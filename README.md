1. 🧠 Interpretation Over Objective Reality

The game world isn’t meant to be “understood” in a literal way. Like Zork, traditional games simulate reality—but abstract ones invite personal interpretation instead.

2. 🎭 Symbolism as Core Language

Everything represents something beyond itself. A shape, object, or space may reflect emotions or ideas, similar to surreal works like The Persistence of Memory.

3. 🌀 Ambiguity is Intentional

Unclear meaning is not a flaw—it’s the design. Inspired by ideas in Phenomenology, the player’s experience creates meaning.

4. 🔁 Nonlinear & Recursive Structure

Events may loop, repeat, or subtly change, creating a dreamlike flow rather than a fixed storyline.

5. 🗣️ Language is the World

The text itself is the environment—style, tone, and wording shape reality, much like experimental narratives such as House of Leaves.
